Sin

Sin is just a woman with a troubled past, and an eagerness to please Satan. It took an emotional toll on her, when Satan forgot about her and their relationship, and her desire to make up for lost time allows her to be more susceptible to his wishes. As described in Book II, Sin is a woman from the waist up, and a serpent from the waist down. Considering how Satan sees himself in her, the woman half of Sin is the female version of Satan who is also extremely attractive. She is clothed in a standard black crop top, snakeskin pants, golden boots, and a statement belt made from black poodle hair. This belt is representative of the dogs gnawing at her stomach, but takes away the violent and sexual imagery of this punishment.
